<span id="about"></span>

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of California, Davis, working in the Visualization and Intelligence Augmentation (VIA) Lab. My research focuses on information visualization, visual analytics, multimodal interaction, context-aware prediction, and AI-assisted analytical workflows.

My recent work includes context-aware intent inference for time-series visual analytics, interpretable spatiotemporal forecasting, multimodal time-series pattern querying with natural language and sketches, and context-aware trajectory prediction.

Before joining UC Davis, I earned my B.Sc. in Computer Science from Georgia Institute of Technology, graduating summa cum laude. I have also worked on intelligent tutoring systems, autonomous vehicle perception with large language models, hate speech propagation modeling, and ethical issues around AI-generated art.

Research Interests
======
- Information visualization and visual analytics
- Context-aware prediction and forecasting
- Multimodal interaction with natural language and sketches
- Large language models for analytical systems
- Interpretable AI and human-centered machine learning
